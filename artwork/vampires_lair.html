Vampire's Lair
I previously wrote an essay on the historical relationship between written prose and illustration, and used gothic and superstitious literature and artistry as a contextual example. This essay was featured on a fold-out poster zine, and this illustration was printed on the back of it.
Narrative illustration, especially when exploring topics such as vampirism and other macabre elements, have always interested me as they root back to the concept of 'death' and what it means in different cultures and contexts to explore mortality, something that all humans are destined to succumb to - grotesquely speaking!
I believe the fear of death is completely rational, as we can only philosophise what happens to our consciousness after we die, but the beautiful media and literature humanity has created surrounding the topic of mortality is something that will still be cultivated for many more centuries to come. Our current existence is a mere fraction in the grand scheme of the universe, but our experiences will be discussed by future generations of explorative minds.
I explored the concept of 'symbolism' in my essay and how many religious and cultural symbols have been diversified and transformed into modern media to visually support a text. This illustration explores a lot of classic horror iconography to support my essay.
