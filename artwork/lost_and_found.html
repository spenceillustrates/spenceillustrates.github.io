Lost and Found
This project was part of a group exhibition which was titled 'Lost and Found' That was held for one night at Secret Space in Digbeth, Birmingham.
This was a personal piece exploring my relationship with being a queer identity, specifically as a transgender person, in an exploitative capitalistic world that benefits from my joy and passion being taken away from my own relationship with my identity.
Personally for me, I feel as though my experience being transgender is deeply spiritual and emotional. When society and the carnage of politics take me at face-value, I feel myself gravitating towards the opulence and wonder of nature and the universe.
Humanity has so many wondrous and beautiful methods of attempting to analyse mystery and existence, whether it be through cultural rituals, religion or folklore. Capitalism and colonialism has ultimately tainted human connectivity and joy as a weapon of control and establishment of patriarchal values where gender diversity challenges the status quo.
I myself am very connected to celtic pagan practices and beliefs, since my connection to the power of nature and the universe has gotten me through a lot of difficult experiences. Self-care and protection of the body and mind are especially important practices to me as a transgender person, when the exclusion of my identity is capitalised off of in the current political climate. I always try to live by the quote 'joy is resistance' because fear curses us with stagnation and surrender. Queer culture and connectivity will never surrender. That's a revolution within itself.
